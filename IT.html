Cloud

Cloud servers came into fruition in the 1960s by Joseph Carl Robnett Locklider, he was a phycologist and computer science that wanted to connect people all over the world. By the 2000s, technology powerhouse companies such as Amazon and Microsoft were introducing their own cloud computing services (Educba).

Modern cloud services allow companies to make resources accessible to people over a remote network or online interface. We may not realise how often we use these things in our everyday life, from school to work and even at home, these services make things such as accessing school resources via a school portal, working remotely, and syncing your information across all your devices. This means that the cloud servers are the heart of any cloud computing service, and they can be delivered in multiple ways, Infrastructure as a Service, Platform as a Service or Software as a Service and Function as a Service, the platform can also be deployed in different ways depending on the enterprise’s preferences. They can be deployed in a public way the third part provider makes the resource available to the general public over the internet, an example of this service is the Google Cloud. It can be also deployed in a private way where an enterprise will use a proprietary architecture and will run the cloud with its own data centre, the enterprise will have their own hardware on premises and have all control of underlying cloud infrastructure. Lastly, they can also be deployed in a hybrid way which combines features of both public and private.

Cloud severs come into existence through a process called virtualisation which makes use of a software called a hypervisor also known as a virtual machine monitor, this software creates and supports multiple virtual machines by creating one host computer capable of running multiple guest virtual machines. It divides and shares the resources of the host computer such as its memory and processors. In a public setting the resources are split and used by multiple organisations and/or individuals, with dedicated private servers, owners or renters are provided with their own physical server which the hypervisor virtualises but does not split the resources.

As technology continues to evolve, these virtual machines will likely become much more secure, with security systems becoming more integrated with AI and machine learning, this will help automate all the processes of threat prevention as well as risk detection. We will also likely find ourselves to become more integrated with technology with things such as Internet of Things (IoT) technology which allows someone to manage all their smart devices from a central hub and Multi-cloud platforms which would allow people to integrate all their cloud providers into one central space. One of the largest growing platforms for the cloud service is serverless computing also known as Function as a Service, this is where developers do not need to manage the cloud server and all they need to do is write and deploy their application code.

The continued development of this sector of technology means we will likely mean we will continue to become more intwined with technology we will see the positive and negatives of this relationship between societies growing dependence on technology. In 2021, Nassim Khadem reported that the Australian Cyber Security Centre received a cybercrime report at least once every 10 minutes which turns out to be approx. 164 a day, we will likely see this number rise with cybercrimes such as malware installation where people might unknowingly download a resource from an unsecure cloud server that contains a computer virus which could possibly lead to these people having personal information stolen which thus may lead to identity theft. This will greatly affect many people in their day to day lives as well as the possibility of these people’s jobs going redundant. Jobs such as accountants, assembly line workers, bank tellers are among some of the careers that are susceptible to the advancements of technology. There are also jobs that will be more sought after such as software developers and Customer Experience Specialists and online teachers as well as some jobs that will need to be instated such as 3D Printer technicians, smart home designers and human-machine team managers.

For people like us, this innovation in the IT sector makes it possible to broaden one’s horizons, even though it currently feels like there are many pathways to go down those pathways will continue to split and new pathways will be created. This should excite people in the IT world with the possibility of creating new things and discovering more about what possibilities there are. The future development of cloud servers will mean that people like me will have easier access to resources needed for things such as school and work and will likely mean that as our careers progress, we will likely be able to work from anywhere in the world. While this might excite people in IT, this might make others worried, as generations continue to change, technology does with it, older generations start feeling more and more out of the loop with the newest tech. They might feel like they do not know how to integrate their lives with the new technology coming out, at least I know that the older generations in my family do. Nevertheless, technology will keep evolving and we will move with it.
